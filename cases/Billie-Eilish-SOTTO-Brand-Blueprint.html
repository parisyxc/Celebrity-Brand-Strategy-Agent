Billie Eilish is a figure built on radical self-preservation — the insistence that the most difficult part is holding on to yourself. Her aesthetic lives in heavy shadows, ASMR-minimal soundscapes, and a dark intimate palette of deep blues, blacks, and silvers. She stands for the right to evolve, hide, and redefine one’s identity away from the public gaze.

Her documented obsession with smell is not a marketing angle — it is a genuine perceptual framework. She experiences music through scent, space through texture, emotion through temperature. “I love scents that have layers that you can’t quite name right away.”

Voo Store — Raw × Refined

The exterior must feel like an object pulled from a Berlin warehouse — matte black anodized aluminum, visible machining marks, a weight that anchors it to a surface. But the interior technology is precision-engineered: nebulizing chambers that atomize scent at the molecular level, Bluetooth sync that maps fragrance release to audio waveforms in real time. The outside says found in a disused factory. The inside says designed by someone who hears in color.

TSUTAYA — Lifestyle Proposal

This is not a “diffuser.” It is a proposal for how a room becomes. Scent cartridges are labeled by state, not fragrance name: “3 AM and the Screen Just Went Dark,” “The Conversation You Almost Had.” Each cartridge evolves over 45 minutes, shifting from top to base like a track moves from verse to coda. You don’t choose a scent — you choose a duration of feeling.

Indie Print — Analog Appreciation

Every cartridge contains one “analog note” — a raw, unprocessed botanical (unrefined vetiver root, actual wood smoke residue, crushed wet earth) sitting beneath the molecular layers like a hand-drawn line beneath a digital grid. Packaging uses matte uncoated black card with blind embossing — text you feel before you read, typography that exists in shadow.

Phase 3 — The Blueprint

Brand Concept

01
Brand Name & Core Logic
The Foundation

SOTTO

Italian: “under / beneath” — as in sotto voce, the whispered voice

“The architecture beneath the room you’re in.”

Everything about Billie Eilish’s aesthetic lives beneath the surface: the whispered vocal beneath the stadium sound system, the unnamed scent layers beneath the legible top note, the identity preserved beneath the public gaze. SOTTO is the brand for the layer you feel before you understand — a music-synced diffuser that transforms audio waveforms into olfactory choreography. The room itself becomes the instrument. You don’t listen to the music — you inhabit it.

02
Hero Product
“Monolith” — Music-Synced Nebulizing Diffuser

3 AM

Almost

Morning Storm

Body

Matte black anodized aluminum cylinder, 180mm × 65mm — proportions of a microphone, weight of an anchor. Bead-blasted surface between silk and sandpaper. A single vertical strip of smoked borosilicate glass reveals the nebulizing chamber: scent atomized into particles so fine they rise like a rumor.

Technology

Bluetooth module reads audio signal from any paired source. Maps scent release to frequency, amplitude, and tempo in real time. The diffuser breathes with the music — exhaling on the downbeat, holding during silence, building through crescendo. No buttons. No screen. The app is the only interface.

Scent Cartridges

“3 AM” — Cold silver aldehydes → bruised fig and black tea → unrefined vetiver with real wood smoke residue.

“Almost” — Rain on warm concrete → crushed iris and white musk → sandalwood and skin accord.

“Morning Storm” — Ozone and salt → black currant bud and wet leaves → damp linen and cedarwood.

Packaging

Matte black rigid box, soft-touch lamination — the kind of black that absorbs light. No external text. Brand name blind-embossed on the lid — you find it by touch. Interior: die-cut cavity lined with charcoal suede. A single card, black on black (text visible only at an angle): “This device does not fill a room. It becomes one.”

03
Core Ritual
The Behavioral Catalyst

【 The Hour You Can’t Name 】

It is not 3 AM but it feels like 3 AM. The lights are off. You are not asleep but you are not awake in the way the world requires. You open the SOTTO app. You choose “3 AM.” You press play on the album. The Monolith begins.

At first you smell nothing. Then — a metallic coolness at the edge of your breath, like standing near a window you haven’t opened yet. As the bass enters, the fig arrives: dark, almost bruised. You close your eyes. The room is no longer a room — it is a temperature, a pressure, a weight on your chest that feels like company. By the time the vetiver reaches you, smoke-edged and rooted, you have forgotten you are smelling anything. The scent has become the architecture. You are inside the song.

Time

Whenever it feels like 3 AM.

Sound

Whatever you can’t stop listening to.

Duration

One cartridge cycle. Or until you fall asleep.

None of these treat scent as a temporal, responsive, emotionally intelligent medium — something that breathes with the moment, evolves with the music, and creates rooms that are not decorated but performed.

06
Retail Space Concept
“The Listening Room”

Location: A basement space in Kreuzberg, Berlin — accessed through a plain steel door at street level with no signage. Only a milled aluminum plate engraved with “SOTTO” at finger height. The descent is part of the experience.

Space & Materials

60 square meters. Walls are raw poured concrete bearing the imprint of wooden formwork. Floor is polished black resin — reflective enough to double the room’s depth, absorbing all sound. Near-zero lighting — illumination comes only from the diffusers’ glass channels (blue-silver glow) and a single recessed LED track at floor level.

Scent Zones

Four zones, each defined by a Monolith running a different cartridge. No walls between zones — the scent itself is the architecture. You cross from “3 AM” to “Almost” by walking, and the room changes around you without anything visible moving.

Sound

A continuously evolving ambient mix synced to the active scent zones. Speakers are embedded in the concrete walls. The sound does not come from a direction. It comes from the room.

Product Display

A single 4-meter raw steel shelf at chest height. Each Monolith lit by a focused pinpoint spot — the only bright light in the space, making each diffuser appear to levitate in darkness. Cartridges arrayed by state, not alphabetically.

At the center of the room, a single Monolith prototype — the first unit ever produced, non-functional, sealed in a block of transparent resin — sits on a concrete pedestal at eye height. Its glass channel still contains the residue of the first test cartridge. A small plate reads: “Prototype 001. Scent residue: Unnamed.” The room smells of it faintly — or you imagine it does. That uncertainty is the point.
