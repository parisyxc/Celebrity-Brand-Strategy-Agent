Troye Sivan is a figure built on controlled contradiction — a YouTube-era diarist who became a stadium-scale pop spectacle without ever losing the sense that you’re watching something intimate. His visual language is Bold Winter: saturated neons, electric jade, vinyl surfaces, high-contrast lighting borrowed equally from the club and the gallery. But underneath the spectacle is a deeply personal, diary-like mode — the camera roll aesthetic, the slightly “off” frame, the beauty found in the rough-hewn.

His queerness is not a subject — it is the grammar of his aesthetic. The Sweat Tour with Charli XCX was a masterclass in queer communal energy: bodies in proximity, shared heat, the transformation that happens when a room full of people decides to stop performing composure.

Phase 3 — The Blueprint

Brand Concept

01
Brand Name & Core Logic
The Foundation

APRÈS

French: “after” — the space between arrival and intimacy, what comes next

“Everything good happens after the first drink.”

Troye Sivan’s entire creative career is built on what happens after the performance of composure drops — the diary entry after the public face, the sweat after the opening song, the real conversation after the small talk. APRÈS is a home-hosting essentials line that designs for the second half of the evening: the moment when the lighting gets lower, the glasses get refilled, and the table becomes the center of something real. It is not party supply. It is the material residue of genuine connection.

02
Hero Product
“Condensation” — Hand-Blown Chromatic Glassware Set

Glass

Hand-blown borosilicate in a proprietary color technique: each glass transitions from deep, saturated color at the base (jade, amber, cobalt, smoke) to clear at the rim — as if the color is rising like heat. Trapped micro-air bubbles scatter light like distant club lasers. Each glass in the set is a different color — not a matching set but a cast of characters.

Form

Slightly bottom-heavy, with a gentle convex curve that fits the palm rather than the fingertips — you hold these. The base has a subtle concave dimple that catches condensation, pooling it into a jewel-like puddle. The water ring it leaves on the table is a perfect circle of color-tinted moisture — the residue of use as decoration.

Tactile Feel

Warm to the touch — borosilicate retains the temperature of whatever it holds. The surface has a barely perceptible texture, like touching a pane of glass in a room where someone has been breathing.

Packaging

Nested in a molded recycled pulp tray (matte black, raw texture). Outer sleeve in heavy stock, printed in a single neon spot color (jade) on black — Risograph energy. Includes a folded card with a playlist QR code: Troye-curated music for “the second half of the evening.” Inside: “A glass is just a room you can hold.”

03
Core Ritual
The Behavioral Catalyst

【 The Second Pour 】

APRÈS does not design for the arrival. It designs for the moment after everyone has settled — coats off, shoes optional, the first drink gone and the second being poured without anyone asking. The table has become the center of gravity. Someone has moved the candle closer. The music has shifted from background to something someone chose on purpose.

You pour into the Condensation glasses and the color at the base catches the low light — four different hues around a table, like stained glass in a chapel built for Friday night. The linen runner beneath has gathered creases from elbows and set-down plates. No one is performing. The room is its own event.

Time

10:30 PM onward.

Sound

The playlist has found its groove — no one is skipping tracks.

Duration

Until someone says “one more” and means it.

04
Market Gap
The Unoccupied Niche

Mode A: The registry default — “good glasses” that whisper affluence and say nothing. Mode B: The Instagram tablescape — photographed before anyone sits down, optimized for content not contact. Mode C: The disposable — Solo cups, Amazon basics, objects that announce “I don’t care.”

None of these modes serve the queer dinner party, the late-night kitchen gathering, the “come over, I’m cooking” text that turns into a five-hour conversation. There are no hosting objects designed for intimacy at close range.

06
Retail Space Concept
“The Pre-Game”

Location: A basement-level space in Berlin-Kreuzberg or a Bushwick storefront in Brooklyn — the neighborhood’s DIY/queer energy over Manhattan’s polish.

Space & Materials

Enter below street level — the descent signals transition from public to private. Low ceiling. Walls are raw plaster, painted matte black from waist-height down (the “sweat line”). Above: exposed plaster, cracked and imperfect. Pendant lamps in hand-blown glass cast warm, color-saturated pools. Permanently set at 10:30 PM lighting.

Experience

A long communal table runs the center — set with APRÈS glassware, linens, and lighting. Always set for a dinner about to happen. Visitors sit, hold the glasses, feel the weight. Product is not on shelves — it is in use on the table.

The Bar

Along one wall: a functioning bar, minimal — three bottles, ice, a stack of Condensation glasses. Staff pour drinks. The transaction is secondary to the experience of using the product in context: a room, a table, other people, a drink.

Atmosphere

Scent: bergamot, warm cedar, a trace of cigarette smoke that might be imagined. The smell of a good night. Sound: a playlist that starts when the first visitor enters and never repeats.

The bathroom mirror has a single line hand-painted at eye level in neon jade: “You look like someone I’d stay out late with.” You only see it when you wash your hands. It is the only text in the entire space.
