## Phase 1: Deep Profile

**Aesthetic DNA:**
Troye Sivan is a figure built on controlled contradiction — a YouTube-era diarist who became a stadium-scale pop spectacle without ever losing the sense that you're watching something intimate. His visual language is Bold Winter: saturated neons, electric jade, vinyl surfaces, high-contrast lighting borrowed equally from the club and the gallery. But underneath the spectacle is a deeply personal, diary-like mode — the camera roll aesthetic, the slightly "off" frame, the beauty found in the rough-hewn. He is not interested in perfection. He is interested in *presence*.

His queerness is not a subject — it is the *grammar* of his aesthetic. The Sweat Tour with Charli XCX was a masterclass in queer communal energy: bodies in proximity, shared heat, the transformation that happens when a room full of people decides to stop performing composure.

**Core Values:**
1. **Radical Authenticity** — grew up on YouTube, foundation is diary-like intimacy with audience. "Creating for yourself" as the only sustainable long-term mode.
2. **The Human Element** — vocal critic of sterile perfection. Celebrates the hand of the creator: rough finishes, personal camera rolls, the slightly "off" aesthetic that feels lived-in.

**Persona Adjectives:** Electric. Intimate. Subversive.

### Tension Worth Exploiting
Troye Sivan built his career on making the intimate feel electric and the electric feel intimate — and the design world is now collapsing the boundary between "nightlife venue" and "home," creating a perfect opening for hosting objects that carry the energy of a night out into the architecture of staying in.

## Phase 2: The "Edit" Scan

### Aesthetic Movement: 【Sweating Glass (汗のガラス)】

**Voo Store Filter — Raw × Refined:**
Sivan's tension is not between industrial and luxury — it's between *sweat* and *glass*. The raw element is the body: heat, proximity, the condensation ring a drink leaves on a surface, the imperfect mark of human use. The refined element is the vessel: blown glass catching neon light, a surface so polished it becomes a mirror for the room. For hosting objects, this means: glassware that looks like it emerged from a Kreuzberg glassblowing studio at 3 AM — color-saturated, slightly asymmetric, with trapped air bubbles that catch light like a club's laser grid. Linens that are not crisp but *warm* — the temperature of a room after people have been in it for hours.

**TSUTAYA Filter — Lifestyle Proposal:**
This is not "entertaining" — it is the *architecture of proximity*. The TSUTAYA logic here: these objects are not organized by product type but by **social choreography**. A set of glasses is not "a set of four glasses." It is "The First Round" — the opening act of an evening. A linen runner is not a table covering — it is "The Surface Where You Set Things Down When the Conversation Gets Good." Each object is a stage direction for intimacy.

**Indie Print Filter — Analog Appreciation:**
Troye's YouTube origins are the ultimate indie print credential — the handmade, the diary-like, the camera-roll aesthetic. This brand should feel like it was documented by a friend with a film camera at 1 AM: slightly out of focus, color-saturated, the warmth of a room full of people you chose to be with. Packaging should reference zine culture — Risograph-adjacent printing, hand-numbered editions, the feeling of receiving something that was made *for* the people who would find it, not for everyone.

## Phase 3: The Blueprint

### 1. Brand Name & Core Logic

- **Brand Name:** **APRÈS**
- **Core Statement:** *"Everything good happens after the first drink."*
- **Brand Logic:** *Après* — the French word for "after," the cultural shorthand for what comes next, the space between arrival and intimacy. Troye Sivan's entire creative career is built on what happens *after* the performance of composure drops — the diary entry after the public face, the sweat after the opening song, the real conversation after the small talk. APRÈS is a home-hosting essentials line that designs for **the second half of the evening**: the moment when the lighting gets lower, the glasses get refilled, and the table becomes the center of something real. Every object is calibrated for proximity, warmth, and the specific beauty of a room that has been *lived in* for a few hours. It is not party supply. It is the material residue of genuine connection.

### 2. Hero Product

**"Condensation" — Hand-Blown Chromatic Glassware Set (4 Glasses)**

**Glass:**
Hand-blown borosilicate glass in a proprietary color technique: each glass transitions from deep, saturated color at the base (electric jade, amber, cobalt, smoke) to clear at the rim — as if the color is *rising* like heat. The walls are intentionally varied in thickness: hold one up to light and the color shifts in density. Trapped micro-air bubbles scatter light like distant club lasers. Each glass in the set is a different color — they are not a matching set but a *cast of characters*.

**Form:**
Slightly bottom-heavy, with a gentle convex curve that fits the palm rather than the fingertips — you *hold* these, you don't pinch them. The base has a subtle concave dimple (the "thumbprint") that catches condensation and pools it into a small, jewel-like puddle when you lift the glass. The water ring it leaves on the table is a perfect circle of color-tinted moisture — the residue of use as decoration.

**Tactile Feel:**
The glass is warm to the touch — borosilicate retains the temperature of whatever it holds. The surface has a barely perceptible texture, like touching a pane of glass in a room where someone has been breathing.

**Packaging:**
Four glasses nested in a molded recycled pulp tray (matte black, raw texture — Voo Store materiality). Each glass sits in its own depression. The outer sleeve is a heavy-stock wrap printed in a single neon spot color (jade green) on black — Risograph energy. The inside of the sleeve carries a single line in sans-serif: *"A glass is just a room you can hold."* The set includes a folded card with a playlist QR code — Troye-curated music for "the second half of the evening."

### 3. Core Ritual / Scenario

**【The Second Pour】**

APRÈS does not design for the arrival. It designs for the moment *after* everyone has settled — coats off, shoes optional, the first drink gone and the second being poured without anyone asking. The table has become the center of gravity. Someone has moved the candle closer. The music has shifted from background to something someone chose on purpose.

You pour into the Condensation glasses and the color at the base catches the low light — four different hues around a table, like stained glass in a chapel built for Friday night. The linen runner beneath has gathered creases from elbows and set-down plates. No one is performing. The room is its own event.

**Time:** 10:30 PM onward.
**Sound:** The playlist has found its groove — no one is skipping tracks anymore.
**Duration:** Until someone says "one more" and means it.

### 4. Market Gap

**Current Landscape:**
Home entertaining products exist in three tired modes. **Mode A:** The registry default — Crate & Barrel, Williams-Sonoma, "good glasses" that whisper affluence and say nothing. **Mode B:** The Instagram tablescape — Loewe-adjacent, perfectly styled, photographed before anyone sits down, optimized for content not contact. **Mode C:** The disposable — Solo cups, Amazon basics, objects that announce "I don't care about this" in a way that kills the mood before it starts.

None of these modes serve the queer dinner party, the late-night kitchen gathering, the "come over, I'm cooking" text that turns into a five-hour conversation. There are no hosting objects designed for intimacy at close range — for the *feeling* of a room, not just the look.

**The Gap:**
Between Iittala (beautiful, cold, Scandinavian restraint) and Urban Outfitters (party-coded, disposable). No one is making hosting objects that carry the *energy* of nightlife into domestic space — the bold color, the sensory warmth, the slight subversion of "proper" entertaining — while maintaining genuine material quality. APRÈS occupies the space where the bar meets the dining table, where "going out" energy is redirected inward.

**Why Troye Sivan:**
He is the artist who made queerness feel like a party invitation rather than a political statement — who demonstrated that intimacy and spectacle can coexist in the same room. His YouTube-to-arena trajectory proves he understands both scales: the two-person conversation and the thousand-person communion. His Sweat Tour was literally a masterclass in *what happens when people share space and heat*. No one is more credible to design the objects that facilitate that transfer — from the venue to the home.

### 6. Retail Space Concept

**Location Archetype:** A basement-level space in Berlin-Kreuzberg (Oranienstraße vicinity) or a Bushwick storefront in Brooklyn — the neighborhood's DIY/queer energy over Manhattan's polish.

**Space — "The Pre-Game":**
You enter below street level, down a short staircase — the spatial descent signals transition from public to private. The ceiling is low. Walls are raw plaster, painted matte black from waist-height down (the "sweat line"). Above: exposed plaster in its original state, cracked and imperfect. Lighting is everything: pendant lamps in hand-blown glass (APRÈS prototypes) cast warm, color-saturated pools across surfaces. There is no overhead fluorescent. The space is permanently set at 10:30 PM lighting.

A long communal table runs the center of the space — set with APRÈS glassware, linens, and lighting. It is always set for a dinner that is about to happen. Visitors are invited to sit, to hold the glasses, to feel the weight and warmth. Product is not displayed on shelves — it is *in use* on the table.

Along one wall: a functioning bar, minimal — three bottles, ice, a stack of Condensation glasses. Staff pour drinks for visitors. The transaction is secondary to the experience of *using* the product in its intended context: a room, a table, other people, a drink.

**Signature Detail:**
The bathroom mirror has a single line hand-painted at eye level in neon jade: *"You look like someone I'd stay out late with."* You only see it when you wash your hands. It is the only text in the entire space.

**Scent:** Bergamot, warm cedar, a trace of cigarette smoke that might be imagined. The smell of a good night.
